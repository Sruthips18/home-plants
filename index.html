Understand Your Plant's Needs

Every plant is unique in its water requirements.While succulents and cacti prefer infrequent watering, ferns and tropical plants need consiste ntly moist soil. Research your plant type to unde rstand its specific needs.
Light is one of the most important aspects of plant care. Some plants thrive in direct sunlight, like succulents and cacti, while others, such as ferns and peace lilies, prefer indirect or low light conditions.

Check the Soil Before Watering

Before reaching for the watering can, check the soil’s moisture level:
Insert your finger 1-2 inches into the soil.
If it feels dry, it’s time to water.
If it’s still damp, wait a day or two.
Watering is another critical factor, and it's important to tailor your approach to the plant's requirements. Overwatering is a common mistake that leads to root rot, characterized by yellowing leaves and soggy soil. Conversely, underwatering results in wilting and dry, crispy leaves. Check the soil's moisture before watering, ensuring it's appropriate for the plant species.
